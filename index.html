Biography

Khanh Nguyen is a postdoctoral researcher at the National Research Laboratory for Biomimetics and Intelligent Microsystems, Konkuk University (KU), South Korea. His research is currently supported by the Brain Korea 21 (BK21) Scholarship, a government-funded program for outstanding research scholars in Korea, and a grant from the Korea Research Institute for Defense Technology Planning and Advancement (KRIT), funded by the Defense Acquisition Program Administration (DAPA). Prior to his master's and PhD studies at KU, Nguyen obtained a Bachelor of Engineering degree from Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT), Vietnam. At HCMUT, he completed a highly selective 5-year Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering program jointly established by the French and Vietnamese governments, which delivers a high-quality engineering curriculum totaling 274 European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS) credits. This program is designed to train excellent engineers in Vietnam, is accredited by the Commission des Titres d’Ingénieur (CTI) in France, and is authorized by the European Network for Accreditation of Engineering Education (ENAEE) to award the European Accredited Engineer (EUR-ACE) label.

Nguyen received the 2026 Spring Academic Award for Best Ph.D. Research Achievement from the College of Engineering at KU. He was selected as one of five nationwide awardees for the Excellent PhD Dissertation Award in the Bioengineering Division from the Korean Society of Mechanical Engineers (KSME) in the same year. In the 2013 Vietnam National University Entrance Examination, Nguyen ranked in the top 1% nationwide and achieved first place in the Natural Sciences Honors Program (track of Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry, achieving a score of 25/30), earning the Quintessential Student distinction at Tran Phu High School, Vietnam.

Nguyen's research focuses on the insect-like flapping-wing robots and tail-beat underwater robotic fish. He has published 7 peer-reviewed journals, including 2 second-authored articles during his bachelor’s studies; 2 first-authored articles during his master’s studies; and 3 publications during his PhD (2 first-authored and 1 second-authored). As part of his doctoral studies, he has 2 additional manuscripts currently under review.

Beyond his research, Nguyen leads several student groups at HCMUT working on bioinspired locomotion. His goal is to help students understand motion principles in nature through simulations and to encourage them to further develop and apply these concepts in unmanned vehicle systems. He welcomes opportunities for collaboration and partnerships in related fields.
